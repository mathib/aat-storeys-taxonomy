Abstract

The AAT-STOREYS taxonomy contains OWL classes to classify **building storeys** via subclasses of [bot:Storey](https://w3id.org/bot#Storey). The taxonomy concepts are extracted and restructured from the Getty Art & Architecture Thesaurus ([Getty AAT](https://www.getty.edu/research/tools/vocabularies/aat/about.html)), v2020-07-29. Other taxonomies might define classification concepts for building sites, buildings, and spaces. The taxonomy is structured using RDFS subsumption axioms.

The namespace for AAT-STOREYS terms is https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/aat-storeys#

The suggested prefix for the AAT-STOREYS taxonomy is aat-storeys

The Turtle version of the AAT-STOREYS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/aat-storeys/aat-storeys.ttl
The RDF/XML version of the AAT-STOREYS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/aat-storeys/aat-storeys.rdf
The JSON-LD version of the AAT-STOREYS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/aat-storeys/aat-storeys.jsonld
The N-Triples version of the AAT-STOREYS taxonomy is available at https://w3id.org/con-tax/zones/aat-storeys/aat-storeys.nt

The AAT-STOREYS taxonomy was created by Mathias Bonduel (KU Leuven) during his PhD research (2017-2020) funded by the Research Foundation Flanders in the form of a Strategic Basic Research grant (grant agreement 1S65917N).
